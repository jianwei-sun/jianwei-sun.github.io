I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Bionics Lab at the University of California, Los Angeles, under the supervision of Prof. Jacob Rosen. My research focuses on the control and estimation of an exoskeleton robot for physical human-robot interaction, in the context of automated physical rehabilitation, and also includes:

  • Generating virtual dynamics through admittance control for robotic systems
  • Safety, pose estimation, and sensor fusion for physical human-robot interaction
  • Feedback linearization-based control of nonlinear dynamics

My industry experience consists of one motion-planning and control internship at Skydio (Autonomy), two electrical engineering internships at Apple (Wearable Systems), and one software engineering internship at Intel (Programmable Solutions). My internship skillset includes robot control, software development, circuit design, and hardware validation.
